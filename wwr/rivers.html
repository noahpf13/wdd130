Rivers We Run

From free-flowing wilderness runs to world-famous canyon rapids, Dry Oar guides unforgettable rafting adventures on some of the most scenic rivers in the West.

Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

Nestled deep in the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return, the Salmon River winds through rugged mountains and sandy beach campsites. Towering ponderosa pines provide shade while elk, deer, bear, and bighorn sheep are often seen along the banks.

As one of the longest undammed rivers in the United States, the Salmon changes character with the seasons: high, fast, and chilly in early summer; lower, slower, and warmer later in the year.

  • Rapid rating: Class I–IV
  • Great for families and adventurous beginners
  • Best for swimming in late summer’s warmer water

Desolation & Gray Canyon, Utah

Winding through some of the most remote country in central Utah, the Green River carves deep through high gray cliffs and colorful desert mesas on its way toward the Colorado River.

This 84-mile stretch from Sand Wash to Swaseys Rapid features beautiful scenery, fascinating geology, and evidence of both prehistoric and historic cultures along the canyon walls.

  • Rapid rating: Class II–III
  • Typical trip length: 6 days
  • Designated National Historic Landmark

Grand Canyon, Arizona

The legendary Grand Canyon is the granddaddy of all river trips. Towering canyon walls, hidden side canyons, waterfalls, and ancient ruins combine with powerful whitewater for a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Rapids here are rated on a unique Grand Canyon scale from I–X (1–10) to cover the huge variety of water features. This roughly maps to the more familiar Class I–V system used elsewhere.

  • Grand Canyon rapid scale: I–X (1–10)
  • World-class multi-day expedition
  • Access to side hikes and hidden waterfalls only by river
